The Exam

What is on the 2026 Digital SAT

The Digital SAT is a computer-adaptive exam. The questions you see in the second module of each section depend on how well you do in the first. Understanding this structure changes how you prepare.

Section Time Questions Format
Reading & Writing 64 minutes 54 questions Two 32-minute modules
Math 70 minutes 44 questions Two 35-minute modules
Total 134 minutes 98 questions Four modules total
Math Topics Covered

Heart of Algebra (linear equations, systems, inequalities)  ·  Problem Solving and Data Analysis (ratios, percentages, graphs)  ·  Advanced Math (quadratics, exponentials, polynomials)  ·  Additional Topics (geometry, trigonometry, area and volume)

The new objective is not just accuracy but also efficiency. The exam rewards students who can find the most direct route to a solution. We teach you when to use your calculator and when working by hand is faster.

Sessions

How the sessions are structured

This program is open to incoming 11th and 12th graders. Sessions 1 and 2 are comprehensive deep-dives, and Session 3 is a focused execution sprint.

Sessions 1 & 2 — Foundation

June 1 – July 1   |   July 13 – August 7

These are full, comprehensive 4-week sessions that break down the SAT section by section. Whether you are building your foundation from scratch or refining what you already know, we cover every concept, strategy, and approach you need. Each week has a focused topic, and we move through Reading, Writing, and all areas of Math in a structured way.

  • Weekly Rhythm 3 main cohort lectures to learn the logic and strategy, plus 2 small-group Squad Huddles for collaborative problem-solving and personalized feedback.
  • Capstone Week The final week of each session is a Full Simulation Week. We transition from learning to doing, taking timed practice tests together so you can see strategies applied in real time.
  • Outside Work Work is assigned by end of day Wednesday each week. There are no rigid deadlines, but completing it before Monday gives me a chance to review your work and offer personalized feedback before the new week starts.
  • Session 2 Plus Students returning from Session 1 are re-evaluated and placed in a group that reflects their progress. You will not see repeated work, you will see deeper applications to push you further.

Session 3 — The Final Grind

August 10 – August 21

This is the execution phase. While Sessions 1 and 2 focus on learning and understanding concepts, Session 3 is entirely about doing. In this 2-week sprint, we drop the theory and focus 100 percent on test-taking simulations and building exam-day stamina. This session is recommended for everyone, including students who completed Sessions 1 and 2.

  • Targeted Every session focuses solely on implementing strategies under real SAT-like conditions. No new material, just execution.
  • The Grind Multiple timed practice tests, detailed error analysis, and clearing up any final misunderstandings so you walk in feeling ready and confident.
Weekly Schedule

What a typical week looks like

Students meet Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday. Each week follows the same rhythm so you always know what to expect.

  • Mon – Wed Main Cohort Lecture from 12:00 to 1:30 PM. All students attend together. This is where I introduce the week's topic, walk through the strategies, and model the logic step by step.
  • 1:30 – 1:45 Scheduled break. Step away, reset, and get ready for your squad huddle.
  • Huddles Two Squad Huddles per week (60 minutes each) at assigned times based on your group. This is where the smaller cohort works through problems together, asks questions, and gets direct feedback.
  • Outside Practice problems and assignments posted by Wednesday. Complete at your own pace before Monday. I am available throughout the week for questions and check-ins.
  • Time Total About 6.5 to 7 hours of live sessions per week. The rest is up to how much you put into the outside work.
Requirements

What you will need

  • Device A laptop or desktop with a working camera and microphone. All sessions are held via video conference.
  • Internet A stable connection that can support a live video session without cutting out.
  • Bluebook App The official College Board Bluebook app, installed and ready to go. We practice directly in the test interface.
  • Cameras On We ask that cameras stay on during all sessions. It keeps the energy going and creates a real learning environment for everyone in the group.
  • Notes Something to take notes with. This is a strategy-heavy program and you will want to write things down.
Commitment

What we ask of students and parents

This program works when students show up and engage. Here is what that looks like from both sides.

For Students
  • Show Up Attend all main lectures and squad huddles. Your group depends on you being there and you will fall behind without it.
  • Stay Present Cameras on, active in the chat, contributing to squad discussions. This is a collaborative environment, not a passive one.

  • Ask Questions This is a judgment-free space. If something is unclear, ask. That is exactly what the squad huddles and my availability throughout the week are for.
For Parents
  • Payment Tuition is paid in full before the session start date. This confirms your student's spot and keeps the program running smoothly.
  • Schedule Please support your student in keeping the summer schedule. Consistent attendance makes a real difference in what they are able to absorb.

After the Summer

You leave with more than a score.

When you finish the program, you do not just walk away. Every student receives two things to keep the momentum going on their own.

  • Progress Note A personalized summary of observations tracked throughout your time in the program. This includes your strengths, the areas where you grew the most, patterns in your mistakes, and anything worth keeping in mind as you continue preparing. It is a real record of your summer, not a generic report.
  • Study Guide A personalized study plan tailored to where you are when you leave. This tells you exactly where to focus your energy after the program ends, what to practice, and how to keep building on what you learned. You will not be guessing at what to do next.
How to Join

The 4-step enrollment path

  1. Application

    Submit the placement application. We ask about your current math coursework, any prior SAT experience, your goal score, and your biggest challenges with the test. This helps us understand where you are starting from.

  2. Squad Placement

    Students are placed into small cohort groups based on grade level and math background. Every application is reviewed personally. Group sizes vary based on enrollment, and we re-evaluate placements at the start of Session 2 so students are always in the right group for their level.

  3. Agreement and Payment

    Accepted students and parents electronically sign the Student and Parent Program Agreement, which covers attendance expectations, our no-refund policy, and participation standards. Tuition is paid in full to confirm enrollment.

  4. Onboarding

    Once confirmed, students get access to the program Google Classroom where all materials, assignments, and resources live, and are added to the communication hub we use throughout the summer.
